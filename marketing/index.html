A friendly note

Allegro is a one-person side project, so it skips Apple's $99/year signing fee. Steps iii and iv are what macOS asks for in its place — quick, one-time, under a minute total. Thanks for going the extra two clicks.

iii.

First launch: get past Gatekeeper

Sonoma (macOS 14): right-click Allegro in Applications, choose Open, then confirm in the dialog.

Sequoia / Tahoe (15+): double-click Allegro and dismiss the block. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, then scroll all the way down to the Security section — you'll see "Allegro" was blocked to protect your Mac with an Open Anyway button next to it. Click it and confirm.

iv.

Grant Accessibility permission

The first time Allegro launches it asks for Accessibility access (it needs this to read your text selection). Click Open System Settings in the prompt — you'll land on Privacy & Security → Accessibility with an Allegro row waiting. Toggle it on.

That's it — the menu-bar icon stays put, your hotkey is live. Select any text, double-tap ⌘⇧R, read.
