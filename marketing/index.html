The mind processes words faster than the eye can move between them. Speedread eliminates the saccade by exposing the reader to one word at a time at the reader's…
02

Double-tap the hotkey

Default is ⌘⇧R tapped twice within 400ms — rebindable in Settings. A floating panel appears near your cursor.

⌘+⇧+R × 2
03

Read

Words flash one at a time at 200–900 WPM with the optimal recognition point highlighted. Space to pause, arrows to scrub, Esc to dismiss.

Install in under a minute.

The build isn't signed with a paid Apple Developer ID, so macOS will ask you to vouch for it once. Step 3 is the only fiddly bit.

i.

Download the DMG

Grab Speedread-0.1.0.dmg from the latest release. Universal binary — runs natively on Apple Silicon and Intel.

⏷
.dmg
ii.

Drag Speedread into Applications

Open the DMG. The window shows the Speedread icon and an Applications shortcut — drag one onto the other.

iii.

First launch: get past Gatekeeper

Ventura / Sonoma: right-click Speedread in Applications, choose Open, then confirm in the dialog.

Sequoia / Tahoe (15+): double-click, dismiss the block, then visit System Settings → Privacy & Security and click Open Anyway.

iv.

Grant Accessibility permission

Speedread needs Accessibility to read your current selection — without it the panel opens empty. Toggle it on under System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility.

That's it — the menu-bar icon appears and your hotkey is live. Select text, double-tap ⌘⇧R, and read.
